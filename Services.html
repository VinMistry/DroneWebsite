Saftey and Law

Dronerz aims to provide you with the correct information regarding the laws and saftey of drones. Below are summary of saftey and the laws enforced on drones in the U.K.

Laws

Endangerment

The operation of the aircraft must not endanger anyone or anything.

Drone weight

If your drone weighs over 20kg then you're out of luck - it's only legal to use it in certified "danger areas" such as Parc Aberporth aerodrome in West Wales.

Commercial drones

CAA (Civil Aviation Authority) permission is also required for all flights that are being conducted for aerial work (i.e. in very simple terms, you are getting paid for doing it).

Recording/Camera drones

The 'remote pilot' has the responsibility for satisfying him/herself that the flight can be conducted safely. The aircraft must not be flown:

  • over or within 150 metres of any congested area
  • over or within 150 metres of an organised open-air assembly of more than 1,000 persons;
  • within 50 metres of any vessel, vehicle or structure which is not under the control of the person in charge of the aircraft
  • within 50 metres of any person except during take-off or landing, the aircraft must not be flown within 30 metres of any person except for the person in charge of the aircraft.

Distance from the drone

The aircraft shoukd be kept within visual line of sight(normally taken to be within 500 m horizontally and 400 ft vertically) of its remote pilot (i.e. the person controling the drone). Operation beyond these distances must be approved by the CAA (to see if the person is able to safely control the drone).

Note

Laws regarding drones are changing constantly, so we advise customers to regularly check any changes to the law either on this website or the CAA website and for commercial use CAA also has a page.
